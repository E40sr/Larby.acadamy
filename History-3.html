THE 19th AND 20th CENTURIES INTO CENTRAL AFRICA

THE YAO

Origin of the Yao

  • Came from North West Mozambique in the area between Ruvuma and Luambala Rivers.
  • Others theory states that, they came from a mountain called Yao between Mwembe and Luchelingo Ranges in Mozambique.
  • The Yao had a trade connection with the Swahili-Arabs before the came to Malawi.
  • Trade made the Yao wealth which made their neigbours envy them.

Reasons for their migration

  1. Drought and famine

    • When Drought and famine hit the region the Makuwa and Lomwe started raiding the Yao for food. The lomwe were led by Nairuwa
    • The Yao left their homeland in search for a safe area with water and good soil for farming

  2. Confict with the Makuwa

    • The Yao had accumulated a lot of wealth through trade with the swahili-Arabs.
    • This made the Makuwa envy them, as a result attacked the Yao, forcing the to migrate to Malawi.

  3. Maseko Ngoni invasion

    • The Yao were attacked by the Maseko Ngoni who were running away from Shaka in South Africa.[Mfecane]
    • The Yao fled to Malawi

  4. Searching for better trade opportunities

    • The Yao were facingstiff trade completion in their homeland
    • As a result, they migrated to kook for with profitable business
